## Why Women's Health Needs Special Attention

Indian women face a unique set of health challenges that often go unaddressed. Some numbers that should concern all of us:

Nearly 54% of Indian women are anemic, meaning their bodies don't have enough healthy red blood cells. Anemia leads to constant fatigue, weakness, and during pregnancy, it can cause serious complications for both mother and child.

Almost 80% of Indian women experience bone thinning at some point in their lives. This makes them vulnerable to osteoporosis, joint pain, and fractures, especially after menopause.

PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) affects 1 in 5 Indian women of reproductive age, yet many don't even know they have it. Irregular periods, weight gain, acne, and hair thinning are often dismissed as "normal" when they could be signs of a hormonal condition that needs medical attention.

Mental health is another area where Indian women suffer quietly. Stress from managing households, work pressure, relationship challenges, and societal expectations takes a real toll. Yet seeking help for mental health is still stigmatized in most families.

The pattern is clear: Indian women put everyone else first and their own health last. This Women's Day, let's start changing that, one small step at a time.

## 8 Health Essentials Every Woman Should Have

### 1. Iron + Folic Acid Supplements

With more than half of Indian women being anemic, an iron supplement isn't optional. It's essential. Iron helps your body make red blood cells, and folic acid supports cell growth. This combination is especially critical during pregnancy, but even non-pregnant women who experience heavy periods or constant fatigue should consider it.

**When to take:** Daily, preferably with Vitamin C (like orange juice) for better absorption. Avoid taking with tea or coffee as they reduce iron absorption.

### 2. Calcium + Vitamin D

Bone health is one of the most overlooked aspects of women's health in India. After age 30, women start losing bone density gradually. After menopause, this loss accelerates significantly. A daily calcium supplement paired with Vitamin D (which helps your body absorb calcium) can make a real difference.

**Practical tip:** 15-20 minutes of morning sunlight is the best natural source of Vitamin D. But in today's indoor lifestyle, supplementation is often necessary.

### 3. A Trusted Sanitary Product

Menstrual hygiene is directly linked to reproductive health. Using the right sanitary product, whether pads, tampons, or menstrual cups, and changing them regularly (every 4-6 hours for pads) prevents infections. Many women in India still use cloth, which is fine as long as it's properly washed, sun-dried, and stored hygienically.

**What to avoid:** Scented sanitary products. They can disrupt your natural pH balance and cause irritation.

### 4. A Good Multivitamin

Women have different nutritional needs than men. A women-specific multivitamin typically contains higher levels of iron, folic acid, calcium, and B vitamins. It fills the nutritional gaps that are common in Indian diets, especially vegetarian ones.

**Best time to take:** With your largest meal of the day for maximum absorption.

### 5. Antifungal Cream

Fungal infections are extremely common among Indian women, especially during summers and monsoon in cities like Lucknow where humidity is high. Itching, redness, and discomfort in skin folds or intimate areas are often signs of a fungal infection.

Having a basic antifungal cream (like Clotrimazole) at home means you can start treatment early instead of suffering in silence.

**Important:** If symptoms persist beyond a week, consult a doctor. Some infections need oral medication.

### 6. Heating Pad or Hot Water Bottle

Period pain is real. And no, it's not something women should "just deal with." A heating pad applied to the lower abdomen relaxes the uterine muscles and significantly reduces cramp pain. It's a simple, drug-free relief method that every woman should have access to.

**Complementary tip:** Pairing heat therapy with a mild painkiller (like Mefenamic acid, on doctor's advice) works better than either alone.

### 7. Basic UTI Care Kit

Urinary Tract Infections are the most common bacterial infection among Indian women. Symptoms include burning during urination, frequent urge to urinate, and lower abdominal pain. While antibiotics (prescribed by a doctor) are needed for treatment, having ORS packets and a urinary alkalizer at home can provide relief while you get to a doctor.

**Prevention basics:** Stay hydrated, don't hold urine for long, and maintain hygiene. Cotton undergarments help too.

### 8. A Health Check Reminder (Not a Product, but the Most Important One)

This isn't something you buy at a pharmacy. But it might be the most important item on this list.

Every woman above 25 should get a basic health checkup done annually. This includes a CBC (Complete Blood Count), thyroid profile, blood sugar, Vitamin D levels, and for women above 30, a Pap smear. Early detection of conditions like thyroid disorders, diabetes, and PCOS can completely change outcomes.

**Reality check:** Most women in India get their first health checkup only when something goes wrong. Don't be that statistic.
