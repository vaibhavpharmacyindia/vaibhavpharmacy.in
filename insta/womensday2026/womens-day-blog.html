Nutrition: The Foundation of Good Health

Women's nutritional needs change throughout life — from puberty and menstruation to pregnancy, breastfeeding, and menopause. Iron deficiency is one of the most common yet overlooked issues, affecting energy levels, immunity, and overall quality of life.

🥬

Iron-Rich Foods

Include spinach, lentils (dal), jaggery (gud), and pomegranate in your daily diet. Pair with vitamin C sources like lemon for better absorption.

🦴

Calcium & Vitamin D

Women over 30 should prioritise milk, curd, ragi, and sesame seeds. Morning sunlight for 15-20 minutes helps with vitamin D synthesis.

💊

Folic Acid

Essential during reproductive years, not just pregnancy. It supports cell growth, heart health, and helps prevent neural tube defects.

💧

Hydration

Aim for 8-10 glasses of water daily. Dehydration can worsen fatigue, headaches, and urinary tract infections — all common in women.

Reproductive & Hormonal Health

Hormonal health affects nearly every aspect of a woman's life — mood, energy, skin, weight, and fertility. Yet many women hesitate to discuss reproductive health openly. Understanding your body is the first step toward empowerment.

Menstrual Health

Irregular, painful, or very heavy periods are not "normal" and deserve medical attention. Track your cycle using a simple diary or app. If you notice patterns of severe pain, excessive bleeding, or missed periods, consult a gynaecologist. Conditions like PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) and endometriosis are increasingly common and highly manageable when detected early.

PCOS Awareness

PCOS affects an estimated 1 in 5 Indian women of reproductive age. Symptoms include irregular periods, weight gain, acne, and excess hair growth. Lifestyle changes — balanced diet, regular exercise, and stress management — form the cornerstone of managing PCOS alongside medical treatment.

Did you know? Early detection and lifestyle modifications can significantly improve outcomes for PCOS. Simple blood tests and an ultrasound can help diagnose the condition. Speak to your doctor if you notice any symptoms.

Mental Health: Breaking the Silence

Women are twice as likely as men to experience anxiety and depression, yet mental health remains stigmatised in many communities. Hormonal fluctuations during menstruation, post-partum, and menopause can intensify emotional challenges. It's important to recognise that seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Simple daily practices can make a significant difference: spending time outdoors, maintaining social connections, setting boundaries, practising deep breathing, and getting 7-8 hours of quality sleep. If feelings of sadness, anxiety, or overwhelm persist for more than two weeks, please reach out to a mental health professional.

Preventive Screenings: Don't Wait for Symptoms

Prevention is always better than cure. Many serious conditions, including breast cancer and cervical cancer, are highly treatable when caught early. Yet a large number of women skip routine screenings either due to lack of awareness or because they feel healthy.

Essential Health Screenings for Women

✓ Blood pressure & blood sugar — Annually after age 20
✓ Complete blood count (CBC) — Check haemoglobin and iron levels yearly
✓ Thyroid panel — Every 2-3 years, especially if fatigued or gaining weight
✓ Breast self-examination — Monthly at home; clinical exam annually after 25
✓ Pap smear — Every 3 years starting at age 21 for cervical cancer screening
✓ Bone density scan — Recommended after age 40, especially post-menopause
✓ Vitamin D & B12 levels — Annually, as deficiency is very common in Indian women

Bone & Joint Health

Osteoporosis affects women disproportionately — especially after menopause when oestrogen levels drop, accelerating bone loss. Building strong bones early and maintaining them throughout life is crucial. Weight-bearing exercises like walking, climbing stairs, and simple strength training go a long way. Don't ignore persistent joint pain or stiffness; early evaluation can prevent long-term mobility issues.

Skin & Hair Care from Within

Healthy skin and hair are often reflections of internal health. Nutritional deficiencies — particularly iron, biotin, zinc, and omega-3 fatty acids — commonly manifest as dull skin, hair fall, and brittle nails. Before spending on expensive treatments, ensure your body is getting the basics right: a balanced diet, adequate water intake, proper sleep, and sun protection.

Small Steps, Big Impact

You don't need to overhaul your entire lifestyle overnight. Small, consistent changes add up over time. Here are a few habits that can make a meaningful difference:

🚶‍♀️

Move Daily

Even a 30-minute walk counts. Regular movement improves heart health, mood, bone density, and hormonal balance.

🥗

Eat the Rainbow

Include colourful fruits and vegetables in every meal. Each colour provides different antioxidants and nutrients your body needs.

😴

Prioritise Sleep

Poor sleep disrupts hormones, weakens immunity, and affects mental health. Maintain a consistent sleep schedule and aim for 7-8 hours.

📋

Schedule Check-ups

Don't wait for symptoms. An annual health check-up is an investment in your future self. Put it on your calendar today.
